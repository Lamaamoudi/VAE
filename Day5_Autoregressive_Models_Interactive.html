Day 5: Autoregressive Models & Normalizing Flows

1. The Core Concept of Autoregressive Models

Autoregressive models simplify joint probability distribution estimation using the chain rule of probability. Instead of predicting everything simultaneously, they predict one feature/pixel/token at a time, conditioned on all previous predictions.

\[ p(x) = p(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_D) = \prod_{i=1}^{D} p(x_i \mid x_1, x_2, \dots, x_{i-1}) \]

This formulation guarantees valid, normalized probability distributions, making likelihood evaluation simple and exact.

2. Maximizing Log-Likelihood

Training autoregressive models relies on maximizing the true log-likelihood of the dataset under parameter set \(\theta\). This minimizes the Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence between the data distribution and your model.

\[ D(P_{data} \parallel P_\theta) = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim P_{data}} \left[ \log \frac{P_{data}(x)}{P_\theta(x)} \right] = \sum_{x} P_{data}(x) \log \frac{P_{data}(x)}{P_\theta(x)} \]

Because the true dataset distribution \(P_{data}\) is constant relative to our model optimization parameters, maximizing log-likelihood simplifies directly to minimizing cross-entropy loss over historical sequences.
